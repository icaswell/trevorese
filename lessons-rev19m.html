Sesowi Lesson Outlines

Lesson 1

  • new_atoms: i (this/he/she/it), mang (person), ting (thing), bi (be), du (do), so (like), ga (good), no (not)
  • new_atom_compounds: noga (bad), biting (condition), noting (nothing)
  • old_atom_compounds: None
  • grammar_points: Basic Nouns (no 'a'/'the'), the verb `bi`, the verb marker `du`, adjectives with `so`, negation with `no`.
  • story_plot: A person states what they are and are not. "i du bi mang. i so ga. i no du bi ting. i ting so ga."

Lesson 2

  • new_atoms: mi (I/me), yu (you), de (of/from), mau (cat), da (big/very), wi (small)
  • new_atom_compounds: demiting (possession), damau (lion), wimang (child), dada (giant)
  • old_atom_compounds: damang (adult), wiwimang (baby), noga so da (very bad)
  • grammar_points: Pronouns (`mi`, `yu`), possession with `de`.
  • story_plot: I describe my big cat and you describe your small child, and we talk about what belongs to each of us.

Lesson 3

  • new_atoms: a (that), lu (place), si (see), yo (have)
  • new_atom_compounds: alu (there), ilu (here), deyo (loss), nosi (hide)
  • old_atom_compounds: manglu (settlement), damanglu (city), wimanglu (village), yodanating (busy)
  • grammar_points: Demonstratives `i` (this) vs `a` (that), the verb `yo` (to have), plurals (contextual).
  • story_plot: I am here and I have a cat. You are there and you have a friend. I see you. You see my cat.

Lesson 4

  • new_atoms: se (talk), e (what/marks question), ba (with/marks command), na (number/marks plural)
  • new_atom_compounds: ese (ask), gase (compliment), ina (they/them), mina (we/us)
  • old_atom_compounds: nogase (criticism), yuna (you all), ana (themyonder), dana (many)
  • grammar_points: Yes/no questions with `e`, commands with `ba`, explicit plurals with `na`.
  • story_plot: I ask you a question. You answer. I tell you to see my cats. We see the cats together.

Lesson 5

  • new_atoms: go, ka (eat), ma (love), tai (time)
  • new_atom_compounds: igo (come), katai (meal), mama (mama), natai (usual)
  • old_atom_compounds: mamang (friend), kalu (restaurant), golu (road), wika (snack), dama (dear), noma (dislike)
  • grammar_points: Verbs without `bi` (e.g. `mi du go`), combining quantifiers and nouns (`e tai?` - when?).
  • story_plot: My friend and I go to a restaurant. We eat a meal. I ask my friend when she will go.

Lesson 6

  • new_atoms: ya (back/marks past), be (front/marks future), tu (to/direction), wa (one)
  • new_atom_compounds: yase (story), betai (future), datu (cardinal direction), wamang (alone)
  • old_atom_compounds: yatai (before), nawa (win), wawe (same), wating (only)
  • grammar_points: Tense markers `ya` (past) and `be` (future). The preposition `tu` for direction/indirect objects.
  • story_plot: I tell a story. Yesterday, I went to the city alone. Tomorrow, I will go with a friend.

Lesson 7

  • new_atoms: o (all/completely), dwe (truth), meng (think), li (energy/feeling)
  • new_atom_compounds: oting (everything), nodwe (falsehood), omeng (decision), gali (happy)
  • old_atom_compounds: omang (everyone), olu (everywhere), dwemeng (belief), dameng (smart), wimeng (stupidity), nogali (sad/hurt)
    • - grammar_points: The quantifier `o` (all). The concept of nouns as verbs (`du meng` - to do a think). - story_plot: Everyone is happy. I think everything is good. I make a decision. I am not sad.

Lesson 8

  • new_atoms: ge (give), bu (seek/try), bo (body), boi (animal)
  • new_atom_compounds: geting (gift), obu (find), subo (sit), wiboi (bug)
  • old_atom_compounds: gesi (show), gese (claim), dabo (fat), boigu (meat), dabauboi (pet)
  • grammar_points: Verbs with direct and indirect objects (`ge ting tu mi`). Relative clauses using `so`.
  • story_plot: I try to find a gift for my friend. I find a pet, a small animal. I show the pet to my friend.

Lesson 9

  • new_atoms: ko (arm/leg), ku (hard), pi (surface), ping (flat)
  • new_atom_compounds: goko (leg), kuting (stone), topi (hat), pingting (board/table)
  • old_atom_compounds: kopau (hand), kudu (work), kupi (hit), pinggu (cloth), pingbo (lie flat), boku (bone)
  • grammar_points: Forming adverbs with `so` (`du go so kwi` - go quickly). Using location nouns as prepositions.
  • story_plot: A man with strong legs is at work. He puts his hat on the table. He lies on the ground.

Lesson 10

  • new_atoms: ta (up/over), su (down), ne (in), pwa (plant)
  • new_atom_compounds: data (sky), sulu (ground), none (empty), dapwa (tree)
  • old_atom_compounds: tating (top), subo (sit), nelu (instead), pwalu (garden), dapwalu (forest)
  • grammar_points: Spatial prepositions (`ta`, `su`, `ne`). `ta` for superlatives (`ta ga` - best).
  • story_plot: A person sits on the ground in a garden. They look up at the sky. A cat is in the tree. It is the best cat.

Lesson 11

  • new_atoms: lo (water), ki (air), bai (light/white), ni (dark/black)
  • new_atom_compounds: logo (swim), kigo (flight), baitai (day), nitai (night)
  • old_atom_compounds: loka (drink), kiboi (bird), bailo (milk), nolo (dry), dalolu (ocean), tabai (sun)
  • grammar_points: Comparatives using `mai` (more).
  • story_plot: A white bird flies in the day. It is bigger than a small cat. At night, it swims in the ocean.

Lesson 12

    - new_atoms: lang (long), kwi (fast), gwa (easy), bwa (opening/mouth) - new_atom_compounds: langboi (snake), kwigo (run), gwadi (slide), baubwa (door) - old_atom_compounds: langtai (longtime), nokwi (slow), nogwa (difficult), tobwa (mouth) - grammar_points: Review of comparatives (`gengmai` - less). - story_plot: A snake is long, but a road is longer. A fast person runs to the door. A slow person finds it difficult.

Lesson 13

    - new_atoms: we (way), dwa (must), nang (can), po (possible) - new_atom_compounds: dawe (important), dwayo (deserve), nangma (cute), opo (any) - old_atom_compounds: ewe (how), wawe (same), laiwe (different), nonang (disallow), dapo (likely), wipo (unlikely) - grammar_points: Modal verbs (`dwa`, `nang`, `po`). - story_plot: A person must go to the city. They can go by car. Maybe they will see a cute cat.

Lesson 14

    - new_atoms: do (two), ti (three), kwa (four), pau (end) - new_atom_compounds: dotwima (bisexual), tisai (triangle), kwasai (square), pauting (conclusion) - old_atom_compounds: nado (number two), tuti (south), tukwa (west), kopau (hand), gokopau (foot), setaipau (weekend) - grammar_points: Using numbers as quantifiers. Verbs acting on other verbs (`yau du go`). - story_plot: Two friends talk for three hours. They see four square things. At the end, they go home.

Lesson 15

    - new_atoms: swe (dirt), gu (stuff), le (official), leng (connect) - new_atom_compounds: sweboi (land animal), boigu (meat), lelu (province), leleng (marriage) - old_atom_compounds: noswe (clean), kagu (food), dalelu (country), noleng (release), langleng (string) - grammar_points: Infinitives and Gerunds (`du kogo so ga` - Walking is good). - story_plot: It is good to be clean. A married couple lives in the country. They like to eat meat, not just food from the dirt.

Lesson 16

  • new_atoms: sai (side), sang (sound), to (head), swa (self)
  • new_atom_compounds: nosai (ball), gasang (music), topi (hat), swase (name)
  • old_atom_compounds: dosai (angle), sangsi (hearing), tobe (face), swadu (become), swameng (meaning), swawe (nature)
  • grammar_points: Parts of Speech (all words are nouns).
  • story_plot: A person with a hat on their head listens to music. They ask themself the meaning of their own name.

Lesson 17

  • new_atoms: kai (start), pu (get), pe (play), pa (result/fruit)
  • new_atom_compounds: kaisekwai (rootword), pumeng (study), peting (toy), lapa (apple)
  • old_atom_compounds: nubi (create), nogapu (theft), pelu (park), payau (depend)
  • grammar_points: Expressing "perfect" tense with `so tai` (e.g. `mi ya du si so tai` - I have seen).
  • story_plot: A student studies at the park. They have studied for a long time. They eat an apple as a result.

Lesson 18

  • new_atoms: gai (male), mu (female), lai (other), geng (against/but)
  • new_atom_compounds: gaida (father), muda (mother), laiting (also), dageng (war)
  • old_atom_compounds: gaiwi (son), muwi (daughter), laise (answer), gengmang (soldier)
  • grammar_points: Expressing habitual actions with `so natai`.
  • story_plot: A father and mother usually talk to their son and daughter. But today, the son gives a different answer.

Lesson 19

  • new_atoms: yang (color), wang (yellow), swi (sweet), dai (use)
  • new_atom_compounds: seyang (dialect), wangkang (gold), wiboiswi (honey), daiting (tool)
  • old_atom_compounds: niyang (black), baiyang (white), wanglayang (orange), swiwiboi (bee)
  • grammar_points: Expressing "just happened" with `swa ya`. Using `dai` for instrumental case.
  • story_plot: A bee just made some sweet honey. A person uses a tool to get the honey. The honey is a yellow-gold color.

Lesson 20

  • grammar_points: Participles (e.g. `lapa so du su` - a fallen apple).
  • story_plot: A person smells something through a hole in the wall. They write a text on paper to a friend who is far away.

Lesson 21

  • grammar_points: Counterfactuals/Conditionals with `bi`.
  • story_plot: If a chicken lays an egg, I will eat it. If the egg were to break, I would be sad.

  • grammar_points: Passive voice (`mang ya du su bailo` - Someone spilled the milk / The milk was spilled).
  • story_plot: A house on a high mountain is where a person lives a good life. The milk was spilled in the narrow kitchen.

  • story_plot: It is May. An insect with six legs and a spider with eight legs are seen. It is raining.
