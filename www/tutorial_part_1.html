The Sesowi Word
Sounds

Sesowi is pronounced just about the way it looks. The technically-minded can look at the Phonolgy tab for details. Just keep in mind that for a multi-syllabic word, don't get scared -- just pronounce it as the combination of its individual syllables!

Atoms

There are under 200 basic words from which all other words are built. These are called atoms. They all have the form consonant+vowel[+optional ng]. The simplest atom would simply have consonant+vowel, e.g. ma, or su. Atoms representing slightly more complex concepts use more use of compound consonants, diphthongs, and the nasal coda, e.g. yau, kwe, or nang.

There are four special atoms that start with a vowel, representing the most basic concepts of this, that, all, and what. These are usually not used in compound words.

Compounds

Most words in Sesowi are compounds, composed of multiple atoms. It is always unambiguous what atoms a compound is made of. Here are some examples:

en gloss
animal animal
snake (long-animal) long-animal
lizard (arm-snake) arm-long-animal
salamander (water-lizard) water-arm-long-animal

If the compound is on the longer side, it can be written with a hyphen to show the etymology better:

en gloss
dog big-good
forest big-plant-place
wolf (forest-dog) big-plant-place--big-good

Atoms themselves have broader meanings than English words, and if they are used by themselves they can be ambiguous. In this way they are more like Chinese characters. For example, arm has the default meaning of arm, but it can also refer to the hand, or the leg, or any limb. If context is not sufficient, you can use the explicit forms or disambiguation forms:

en gloss
limb/arm/leg/hand arm
hand (arm-end) arm-end
leg (go-arm) go-arm
foot (leg-end) go-arm-end
sphere/circle no-side
sphere (3d-ball) body-no-side
circle (flat-ball) flat-no-side

Note to English speakers: A good number of the world's languages, e.g. Greek and Chinese, do not differentiate "hand" and "arm". So this level of ambiguity is quite natural!
